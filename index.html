What is Mobile Integrated Healthcare?

Mobile integrated healthcare (MIH) is a healthcare delivery model that brings medical services outside of traditional healthcare settings like hospitals and clinics. It involves a team-based approach where healthcare professionals, such as paramedics, nurses, and physicians, provide care in various locations such as homes, community centers, workplaces, and even during events or emergencies. The goal is to make healthcare more accessible and convenient for everyone, especially those who may have difficulty getting to a doctor or hospital.

Care Coordination and Continuity

MIH providers prioritize seamless transitions between care settings. They coordinate with primary care providers, specialists, hospitals, and community resources to ensure continuity of care and optimal patient outcomes.

Technology and Telehealth

MIH may incorporate telehealth and digital health tools to enhance communication, monitor patient health remotely, and facilitate virtual consultations. These technologies support efficient care delivery and patient engagement, and help decrease barriers to accessing care especially in rural and underserved areas.

Patient-Centered Care

MIH focuses on meeting patients where they are, whether it's at home, work, or in the community. This approach emphasizes convenience for patients and aims to improve health outcomes by addressing barriers to accessing healthcare.

Team-Based Approach

MIH typically involves interdisciplinary teams that may include paramedics, nurses, physicians, social workers, and other healthcare professionals. These teams collaborate closely to provide holistic care that considers both medical and social determinants of health.

Preventive Care and Chronic Disease Management

MIH places a strong emphasis on preventive care and managing chronic diseases. Providers work proactively to educate patients, monitor their health status, and intervene early to prevent exacerbations and hospitalizations. MIH teams develop personalized care plans tailored to the specific needs of patients, addressing both medical and social determinants of health.
